VMware Validated Solutions

The purpose of VMware-Validated Solutions is to deploy a VMware-designed SDDC to deliver common business use cases.

VMware Validated Solutions (VVS) are prescriptive, end-to-end solution blueprints built on top of VMware Cloud Foundation that combine architecture, configuration, workflows, and lifecycle guidance into a tested and supportable outcome.

A VMware Validated Solution is a known-good way to solve a concrete problem on VCF — designed, tested, and supported as a whole.

They answer a very practical question:

“Given VCF, how do I implement this specific enterprise capability correctly and safely?”

What Does a Validated Solution Contain

A VVS is more than documentation. It typically includes:

Reference Architecture

  • Component topology
  • Trust boundaries
  • Network flows
  • Failure domains
  • Scale assumptions

Prescriptive Configuration

  • Exact settings (not suggestions)
  • Supported variants
  • Guardrails and constraints
  • Explicit “do / don’t” guidance

Operational Workflows

  • Day-0 (deployment)
  • Day-1 (onboarding, enablement)
  • Day-2 (upgrade, scale, recover)

Lifecycle and Upgrade Compatibility

  • How the solution evolves with VCF upgrades
  • What breaks compatibility
  • What is safe to automate

Everything is validated together, not in isolation.

Why

VCF is a platform, not an opinionated solution by default.

Enterprises, however, want outcomes, such as:

  • Secure multi-tenancy
  • Kubernetes at scale
  • Identity-integrated access
  • Backup, DR, or compliance alignment

Without validated solutions:

  • Every customer invents their own pattern
  • Outcomes vary wildly
  • Support becomes guesswork

Validated Solutions reduce design risk and support friction.

How Do VMware Validated Solutions Relate to VCF Components

Layer Role
VCF (core) Provides the integrated platform
Validated Solution Defines how to use the platform
SDDC Manager Enforces lifecycle safety
VCF Automation Enables consumption and Governance
NSX / vSphere / vSAN Provide execution primitives

Here, VCF is a programmable substrate, and Validated Solutions as approved programs.

Typical VMware Validated Solutions

While names and scope evolve, common categories include:

  • Secure Cloud Infrastructure
  • Identity and Access Integration
  • Kubernetes Platform Enablement
  • Multi-tenant Cloud Architecture
  • Business Continuity / DR
  • Compliance-aligned deployments

Each one narrows a broad platform into a specific, repeatable design.

What VMware Validated Solutions Are Not

They are not:

  • Reference “ideas”
  • Whitepapers
  • One-off examples
  • Mandatory architectures

You can deviate—but once you do, you own the risk.

Benefits and Liabilities

For architects and platform owners, Validated Solutions provide:

Pros

  • Faster time to value
  • Reduced design ambiguity
  • Predictable upgrades
  • Clear escalation paths

Cons

  • Less freedom
  • Opinionated choices
  • Slower adoption of bleeding-edge features

This trade-off is deliberate.

Summary

  • VMware Validated Solutions are prescriptive, supported blueprints
  • They sit above VCF, not beside it
  • They reduce risk by constraining choice
  • They align architecture, operations, and lifecycle
  • Deviating is allowed—but unsupported paths are yours to own
