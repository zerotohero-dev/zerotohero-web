Managing Policies in VCF Automation

Governance is one of the key aspects of building a cloud-like experience for an organization. In the 

VCF Automation

 organization portal, application teams can use the self-service catalog and the services console to provision resources depending on their project needs and permissions. As an organization administrator, you can impose guardrails around those provisioning requests. You use 

VCF Automation

 policies to enforce the governance through approval workflows and policy templates. Each 

VCF Automation

 policy is a set of rules or parameters that are applied to 

VCF Automation

 projects to govern deployment requests from application teams, freeing the organization administrator for other tasks.

Policy Types

There are two general categories of policies you can create in 

VCF Automation

:

  • Policies that can be applied to catalog requests.

    • Approval Policy. Approval Policy adds a layer of governance before the requested resources can be deployed, approvers need to provide their acceptance Approval policies control who must approve resource deployment before the request is provisioned. If an approval policy is configured, approvers can view the list under the 

      Inbox

       section for pending requests for their approval. See Configure Approval Policies in VCF Automation.

    • Day 2 Action Policy. Day 2 Action policies control what group of users can run the selected actions on deployed resources. See Configure Day 2 Action Policies in VCF Automation.

    • Lease Policy. Lease policies define the maximum amount of time a deployment can exist. When a deployment expires, it is destroyed and its resources are reclaimed. Lease Policy reduces the need for the organization administrator to intervene manually to reclaim resources, allowing administrators to specify when they want deployed resources to expire. See Configure Lease Policies in VCF Automation.

  • Policies can be applied to IaaS Services requests.

    • IaaS Resource Policy. To govern application teams' requests to provision IaaS services, like Virtual Machine, Kubernetes Cluster, organization administrators can configure IaaS Resource policies to have granular control on the type of provisioned resources and their configurations. IaaS Resource policies offer policy- as-code capability to enforce custom constraints on IaaS resources, beyond the built-in policy templates. Modifying the policy template would need knowledge Common Expression Language (CEL) is used in the Kubernetes API. See Configure IaaS Resource Policies in VCF Automation.

Policy Scope and Criteria

VCF Automation

 policies can be scoped to the entire organization or a selection of projects in the organization. Organization administrators can further refine the policy scope by defining policy criteria. For example, to apply a policy to all the deployments except the deployments named pipeline test, add criteria and construct an expression similar to 

name not equal to pipeline test

. Policy criteria can be based on project name, namespace name, namespace class, and so on. For details on constructing policy criteria, see Configure Deployment Criteria in Policies in VCF Automation.

Organization administrators can configure multiple policies for resources, projects, or namespaces. First, policies are evaluated, ranked, and, where applicable, merged to produce an effective policy.

On the 

Policies

 page, organization administrators can create new policies, delete any policy which is no longer required, view a list of policies defined in the organization, view policy assignments and an enforcement list.

  • **

    Policy Definition.

    ** A list of all the policies defined for your organization. You can view policy details or delete a policy which is no longer required. To create a new policy, click 

    New Policy

     and select one of the policy types.

  • **

    Policy Assignments.

    ** On the Policy Assignment tab, you can view policies that are effectively applied to resources, namespaces, and projects, based on the defined policy criteria.

  • **

    Policy Enforcement

    **. Use the Policy Enforcement tab to audit when policies are applied. It lists enforcement date, policy name, and policy status.

Any policy that you add is applied to current deployments and any new deployments.

To get you started, use the full use cases that are provided for each policy type. The use cases guide you through the process of creating more than one policy. The use case provides contextual explanations of the choices and the desired behavior.

The use cases are followed by more in-depth information about how multiple policies are processed.
