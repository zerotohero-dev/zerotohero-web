Fleet Management

Fleet management in VMware Cloud Foundation is the centralized, policy-driven governance of many infrastructure instances (hosts, clusters, domains, appliances) across their entire lifecycle—from onboarding and entitlement to upgrades, credentials, certificates, configuration drift, and metadata.

What Is a Fleet

A fleet typically includes:

  • Multiple Workload Domains
  • Multiple vCenter Servers
  • NSX Managers & NSX Edge Clusters
  • ESX hosts (often hundreds or thousands)
  • Supporting appliances (SDDC Manager, vSAN, etc.)

Fleet management answers:

“How do we keep all of these consistent, compliant, secure, and up-to-date over years?”

Core Fleet-Management Pillars

License Management

What VCF manages

  • Central license inventory
  • Assignment to:
    • vCenter / ESX
    • NSX
    • vSAN
  • Alignment with subscription usage

Components

  • SDDC Manager
  • VCF Usage Meter
  • VCF Business Services Console

Licenses are fleet-level entitlements, not per-host snowflakes.

VCF ensures:

  • No silent overuse
  • No orphaned entitlements
  • Auditable usage

Lifecycle Management

Owner

  • SDDC Manager

What It controls

  • Bring-up (VCF Day 0 Tasks)
  • Patching and upgrades (VCF Day 2 Tasks)
  • Version skew prevention
  • Compatibility matrices (vSphere, NSX, firmware)

VCF Ensures:

  • Upgrades are coordinated across domains
  • Versions are locked as a bill of materials
  • Drift is actively prevented

You upgrade the fleet, not “a vCenter here and there.”

Identity and Access Management

Layers involved

  • vCenter SSO
  • External IdPs (AD / LDAP / OIDC)
  • Role-based access (RBAC)
  • VCF Automation Organizations and Projects

VCF Ensures

  • Consistent role models
  • Central auth sources
  • Separation of duties across domains

IAM is federated but standardized across the fleet.

No bespoke admin models per domain.

Certificate Management

What Needs Certificates

  • vCenter Server
  • ESX hosts
  • NSX Managers & VCF Edges
  • Internal APIs
  • Trust relationships

Certifacate Challenges:

  • Certificate sprawl
  • Expiration risk
  • Manual rotation = outage risk

VCF Ensures:

  • Centralized visibility via SDDC Manager
  • Supported rotation workflows
  • Integration with enterprise CAs

Certificates are fleet-scale secrets, not local files.

Password and Secret Management

Scope

  • Appliance passwords
  • Service accounts
  • Inter-component credentials
  • Bootstrap secrets

Reality

  • Password rotation must be:
    • Coordinated
    • Non-disruptive
    • Auditable

VCF:

  • Tracks credentials centrally
  • Rotates safely
  • Prevents “unknown admin password” scenarios

This is operational hygiene at scale.

Configuration Management and Drift Control

What Kinds of Configuration

  • Cluster settings (HA, DRS, admission control)
  • NSX transport & policies
  • Storage policies
  • Network constructs
  • Domain-level invariants

Configuration is declared once and enforced everywhere.

VCF:

  • Applies opinionated defaults
  • Detects unsupported deviations
  • Blocks actions that would break fleet integrity

Tag Management

Tags For

  • Automation targeting
  • Chargeback / showback
  • Compliance classification
  • Ownership & environment labeling

Tags provide metadata across the entire fleet.

  • Tags provide horizontal metadata across:
    • Domains
    • Clusters
    • VMs
    • Datastores

“The control plane’s labeling system for infrastructure objects.”

Without tags, automation becomes brittle.

Summary

├── SDDC Manager

│   ├── Credentials
│   ├── Certificates
│   ├── Configuration invariants
│
├── Business Layer
│   ├── Usage Meter
│   └── Business Services Console
│
├── Identity & Access
│   ├── vCenter SSO
│   └── External IdP
│
└── Metadata
    └── Tags & policies
  • Fleet management is the core value proposition of VCF
  • It spans license, lifecycle, identity, secrets, certs, config, and metadata
  • Centralization + opinionation is the feature, not a limitation
  • VCF treats infrastructure as a long-lived, governed fleet, not pets
