VCF Business Services Console

VCF Business Services Console are cloud service that provides the ability to manage VCF Usage, meter appliances, User Roles, and resource access.

VCF Business Services Console (BSC)

VCF Business Services Console (BSC) is the business-facing control plane for VCF that focuses on commercial governance, not infrastructure operations. It provides tenancy, subscription, entitlement, and usage visibility across organizations and projects.

If vCenter answers “what is running?”, BSC answers:

“Who is entitled to what, who is using it, and how should it be charged?”

Where Does BSC Fit in the VCF Picture

VCF
├── Infrastructure & Ops
│   ├── vCenter / ESXi
│   ├── NSX / vSAN
│   └── VCF Automation
│
├── Metering
│   └── Usage Meter
│
└── Business Layer
    └── Business Services Console
        ├── Organizations
        ├── Subscriptions
        ├── Entitlements
        └── Usage visibility
  • Audience: Platform owners, cloud business teams, service providers
  • Not for day-to-day infrastructure operators

What Does BSC Do

Core responsibilities

Business Tenancy

  • Defines Organizations as commercial entities
  • Associates:
    • Subscriptions
    • Entitlements
    • Usage views

This is a business boundary, not a technical one.

Subscription and Entitlement Management

  • Tracks which orgs are entitled to:
    • VCF capacity
    • Optional components (NSX, vSAN, etc.)
  • Aligns commercial contracts with technical consumption

BSC does not provision resources — it governs access.

Usage Visibility

  • Consumes data from VCF Usage Meter
  • Presents usage in:
    • Business-friendly terms
    • Aggregated per org / subscription
  • Enables showback / chargeback workflows

Multi-renant Oversight

Especially relevant for:

  • Internal private clouds with multiple business units
  • Service providers offering VCF-based services

BSC gives a single pane of glass for business accountability.

What BSC Deliberately Does NOT Do

Not its job Why
Provision VMs That’s VCF Automation
Place workloads That’s DRS / vSphere
Enforce quotas That’s VCF Automation / policy
Meter usage That’s VCF Usage Meter
Monitor performance That’s Ops tools

BSC is governance and visibility, not execution.

BSC vs related VCF Components

Component Primary focus
Business Services Console Business governance and entitlements
VCF Usage Meter Raw consumption measurement
VCF Automation Request, approval, provisioning
vCenter Server / NSX Runtime infrastructure
Ops tools Health and performance

Think of BSC as:

The billing and entitlement desk of your private cloud

  • Not the data center.
  • Not the scheduler.
  • Not the automation engine.

Why

With subscription-based licensing:

  • “Installed” ≠ “Billable”
  • “Running” ≠ “Entitled”

BSC:

  • Bridges commercial reality and technical usage
  • Makes private cloud operate more like a public cloud business

Typical users

  • Cloud platform owners
  • Finance / FinOps teams
  • Service provider admins
  • Business unit IT leads

Rarely: day-to-day SREs.

Summary

  • VCF Business Services Console is the business control plane
  • Manages organizations, subscriptions, and entitlements
  • Consumes (but does not generate) usage data
  • Complements Usage Meter and VCF Automation
  • Essential for multi-tenant, subscription-driven VCF deployments
