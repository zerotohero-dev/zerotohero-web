Operations Management

VCF Operations delivers the observability, analytics, and operational intelligence layer for VMware Cloud Foundation environments. It focuses on understanding system behavior, maintaining platform health, and enabling proactive operations across compute, storage, and networking—both in real time and over longer planning horizons.

Where VCF Automation answers “what should be deployed and governed?”,
VCF Operations answers “how is the platform behaving, and where are risks emerging?”

VCF Operations continuously ingests telemetry, events, metrics, and logs from across the VCF stack to provide operators with actionable insights rather than raw data.

Core Capabilities

VCF Health

Provides a holistic, continuously evaluated view of platform health across domains and components.

  • Validates configuration consistency against VMware best practices
  • Detects drift, misconfigurations, and version incompatibilities
  • Surfaces health scores and risk indicators at fleet, domain, and component levels
  • Helps operators move from reactive firefighting to preventive maintenance

This provides a continuous “wellness check” for the entire VCF environment.

Diagnostics

Enables rapid root-cause analysis when issues arise.

  • Correlates symptoms across compute, storage, and networking layers
  • Analyzes historical trends to distinguish transient issues from systemic problems
  • Provides guided troubleshooting workflows and impact analysis
  • Reduces mean time to detect (MTTD) and mean time to resolution (MTTR)

Instead of isolated alerts, diagnostics focus on cause-and-effect relationships.

Log Analysis

Centralizes and contextualizes logs generated across the VCF stack.

  • Aggregates logs from infrastructure and platform components
  • Enables search, filtering, and correlation across time and services
  • Supports security investigations, audits, and post-incident analysis
  • Complements metrics-based monitoring with forensic-level visibility

Logs become evidence, not noise.

Network Operations

Monitors and analyzes the health, performance, and behavior of networking components.

  • Tracks latency, packet loss, throughput, and flow patterns
  • Detects anomalies and deviations from normal traffic behavior
  • Helps identify bottlenecks, misrouted traffic, and configuration issues
  • Provides visibility across logical and physical networking layers

This bridges the traditional gap between virtual networking abstractions and real network behavior.

Storage Operations

Delivers insight into capacity, performance, and reliability of storage resources.

  • Monitors IOPS, latency, throughput, and utilization trends
  • Predicts capacity exhaustion and performance degradation
  • Identifies imbalanced workloads and storage hotspots
  • Supports informed decisions around scaling and optimization

Storage operations turn capacity planning from guesswork into data-driven forecasting.

What VCF Operations Is, and Is Not

VCF Operations is:

  • Observability and operational intelligence
  • Predictive and preventive rather than purely reactive
  • Focused on platform behavior and risk

VCF Operations is not:

  • A workload deployment or provisioning system
  • A policy enforcement or approval engine
  • A tenant-facing self-service layer

Summary

  • VCF Operations is the operational nervous system of VCF
  • It emphasizes insight, correlation, and prediction—not just monitoring
  • It complements VCF Automation by ensuring the platform remains healthy, performant, and secure over time
  • Its value compounds as environments scale and diversify
